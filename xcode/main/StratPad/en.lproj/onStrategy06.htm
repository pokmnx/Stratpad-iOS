On Strategy

Aspiration (1 of 2)

When discussing strategy you'll often hear these words: mission, vision, values and purpose.

Each is important but they often cause confusion because of their overlapping meanings. We've simplified it and use only two words: aspiration and values.

Aspiration
Aspiration is the long-term, over-arching goal that answers the question, "Where do we want our company to be in 10 (or more) years?"

More than just a long range goal, an aspiration should incorporate a

higher sense of purpose. For example, Amazon's aspiration might be:

"We want to be the planet's most customer-centric company and offer an online place where anyone can find anything they might want to buy."

Google's aspiration might be:

"We want to organize all of the world's information and make it universally accessible and useful so that anyone needing to know anything can find it by visiting us online."
