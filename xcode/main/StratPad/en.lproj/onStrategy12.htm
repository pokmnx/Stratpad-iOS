On Strategy

Scope (1 of 3)

The scope of your strategy is the set of boundaries within which you operate and outside of which you will not go.

Scope is often evaluated along multiple dimensions. The four most common are:

  • geography
  • target customers
  • control, and
  • offerings.

These four are now introduced along with examples from Edward Jones, a financial advisory firm.

Geography

  • What geographic markets do we serve and/or operate in?
  • Do we understand the dynamics and requirements of each of these markets (ie. legal, economic, cultural, supplier, etc.)?
  • Do we wish to serve different markets in the future?

Edward Jones works through a network of offices in non-metropolitan centers in North America.
