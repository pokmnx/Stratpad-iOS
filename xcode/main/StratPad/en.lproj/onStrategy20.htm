On Strategy

Goal (1 of 2)

There's no better way to unite your staff, align your activities and create focus among your team than by communicating a single, over-arching, non-financial, medium-term goal.

Several things to note:

  • the word "goal" is singular. In order to get your team on the same page, it's important to select a single goal that everybody understands.
  • the goal should be achievable in the "medium-term". This means different things to different companies. A start-up might select a timeframe of six months. More mature companies will select a timeframe between 2 and 5 years.
  • the goal should be non-financial. While profit and shareholder return are obvious contenders for goals, they do a poor job of communicating to staff where the organization is going. We'll look at financial metrics in the next section.

In the meantime, here are three good examples of non-financial goals:

  • Edward Jones will grow to 17,000 advisors by 2012.
  • University of Leeds will place in the top 50 universities in the world by 2015.
  • Starbucks will have 2,000 stores by 2000.
