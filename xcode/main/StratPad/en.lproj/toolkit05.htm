SWOT

A "SWOT" analysis fully considers the strengths, weaknesses, opportunities and threats faced by your company.

Strategy then determines whether they are important enough to consider (i.e. do we care?) and, if so, how you will respond.

Typically, strengths and weaknesses are internal aspects of your company. Ask yourself, "What do we do well or poorly?"

Opportunities and threats usually arise externally from customers, competitors and regulators.

As you weigh the importance of these items and your potential responses to them, think in longer time-frames (6 months to 3 years) and how it affects your firm's uniqueness and gross profit.
