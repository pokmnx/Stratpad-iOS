Gross Profit vs. Net Income

The bottom-line orientation of business makes it easy to choose net income growth as the primary financial goal of a company. However, this can be difficult or short-sighted. Far better, for many companies, is to focus on growing gross profit.

Focusing on growing net income can often lead to short-sighted and short-term solutions. These solutions often boil down to two options, "reduce expenses" and "grow revenue", which can lead to three problems:

First, cutting expenses is often seen as the fastest way to increase net income. However, it easily leads to short-sighted tactical activity that undermines longer-term strategic initiatives.

Second, growing revenue normally requires an increase in sales and marketing costs which directly conflicts with reducing expenses.

Third, a focus on cutting expenses can keep other options from being fully considered.
