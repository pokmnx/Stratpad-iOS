Gross Profit vs. Net Income

Instead, shift focus to increase gross profit and see what happens.

First, the obvious options change to "grow revenues" and "decrease cost of goods sold". These don't conflict.

Second, increased gross profit provides more cash to invest in expenses. Treating expenses as investments substantially changes perspective: expenses are no longer something to be minimized but investments that must produce a return.

Finally, the shift away from expense-cutting allows other options to surface and be considered.

To illustrate, let's look at two identical companies, each of which has a large customer call center. Each company wants to increase its net income within 12 months.

Company A decides to cut its call center expense. They expect these savings will fall straight to the bottom line.

Company B decides to increase their call center expense. They believe that investing in their customer support center will increase revenues significantly more than their call center investment.
