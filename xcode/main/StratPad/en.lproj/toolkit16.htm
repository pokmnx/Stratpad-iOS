Gross Profit vs. Net Income

Company A cuts their call center expense by 5%.
They freeze wages and shrink staff size through attrition. Naturally, no new investments are made in training programs or equipment. Call durations are monitored and staff are expected to shorten their average call length to compensate for the reduced staff complement.

The result?

  • morale drops
  • customer satisfaction slips
  • up-sell percentage drops
  • new sales close rate drops

Company A is working harder to maintain position.

Company B increases their call center expense by 5%.
Hours of service are expanded, better staff training is provided, and a new self-service website is launched.

The result?

  • morale rises
  • customer satisfaction rises
  • up-sell percentage rises
  • new sales close rate rises
  • they find they can raise their prices on some products

Company B has happier customers, happier staff and a higher net income.
